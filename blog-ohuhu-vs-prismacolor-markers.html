Ohuhu Alcohol Markers — In-Depth Review

The Ohuhu 200 Colour Alcohol Marker Set is one of the best-selling art products on Amazon — and it's easy to see why. For a single affordable price you get 200 colours, a brush tip on one end and a chisel tip on the other, plus the ability to refill your markers when they run dry.

The alcohol-based ink blends beautifully, letting you layer colours and create smooth gradients without harsh lines. Pro tip: Always use Ohuhu markers on marker paper or bleed-proof paper. Regular copy paper will cause the ink to bleed through and feather at the edges.

Prismacolor Premier Markers — In-Depth Review

The Prismacolor Premier Double-Ended Markers are professional-grade alcohol markers that have been trusted by illustrators, graphic designers and comic artists for decades. The ink is rich, vibrant and highly consistent — every single marker delivers the same quality from first stroke to last.

Where Prismacolor stands out is ink depth and tip precision. The fine tip end delivers incredibly controlled linework, while the broad tip covers large areas quickly and evenly. The colour payoff is noticeably more vivid compared to budget alternatives. However, this comes at a significant cost — and the sets top out at 156 colours, far fewer than Ohuhu's 200.

One real drawback: Prismacolor markers are not refillable. When the ink runs out, you buy a new one. For heavy users this adds up fast.

Who Should Buy Prismacolor Markers?

Prismacolor markers are best for intermediate to advanced artists who prioritise ink quality and consistency above all else — and who use a smaller, carefully chosen palette rather than wanting every colour available. Professional illustrators, concept artists and comic creators will appreciate the premium feel. If budget is not a concern and you already know which specific colours you reach for, Prismacolor rewards the investment.

Blending: How Do They Compare?

Both markers use alcohol-based ink, so both blend well. The technique is the same — apply the lighter colour first, then layer the darker colour over it while the ink is still wet to create a smooth gradient. You can also use a colourless blender marker with both brands.

In practice, Prismacolor blends feel slightly more luxurious because the ink flows more smoothly and consistently. But Ohuhu blending is genuinely excellent and most beginners — and even many experienced artists — would struggle to tell the difference in a finished piece.

Price: The Real Difference

This is where the two brands diverge most sharply. A set of 200 Ohuhu markers costs a fraction of what you'd spend on a comparable Prismacolor set — and you'd still end up with fewer colours. If you are a beginner trying to figure out whether you even enjoy working with alcohol markers, spending premium money on Prismacolor before you know your preferences is a big risk. Start with Ohuhu. Upgrade specific colours to Prismacolor later if you feel the need.
