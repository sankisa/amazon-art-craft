What is a Light Pad?

A light pad (also called a light box or light table) is a flat panel with built-in LED lights that shine upward through a frosted surface. When you place your diamond painting canvas on top, the light illuminates the canvas from below — making the printed symbols sharp and easy to read even with tiny drills.

Step-by-Step: How to Use a Light Pad for Diamond Painting

1

Set up your light pad on a flat surface

Place your light pad on a table or desk. Use the included stand to prop it up at a comfortable angle if you prefer working at a tilt. Make sure it is plugged in or fully charged before you start.

2

Turn on the light and choose your brightness

Switch the light pad on and cycle through the brightness levels. Start at a medium brightness and adjust to what feels comfortable for your eyes. You do not always need the brightest setting — medium is often best for long sessions.

3

Place your diamond painting canvas on top

Lay your diamond painting canvas face-up on the light pad. The light will shine through from below, illuminating the symbols on the canvas. Use the included clips to keep the canvas secure if your light pad has them.

4

Peel back a small section of the protective film

Only peel back a small section of the protective film at a time — never the whole canvas. This keeps the adhesive fresh and stops dust and drills from landing in the wrong place.

5

Read the symbols clearly and place your drills

With the light shining through, every symbol on the canvas is now easy to read. Use your diamond pen tool to pick up each drill and place it on the matching symbol. Work section by section from top to bottom.

6

Use the magnifier if needed

Some light pads come with a magnifier attachment. If your canvas has very small symbols or you are working with 3D drills, clip the magnifier on top to make everything even clearer.

💚 Pro Tip: Work in a slightly dimmed room. The contrast between the bright light pad and a darker room makes the symbols even easier to see — and reduces eye strain during long sessions.

⚠️ Important: Never look directly into the light pad for long periods. The LED light is bright and can cause eye strain. Always keep the canvas on top so the light is diffused through it.

Tips for Using a Light Pad Like a Pro

Sort your drills before you start

Organise your diamond drills into a sorting tray before you start. Group them by colour and symbol number. This saves enormous time when you are searching for a specific colour mid-project.

Work section by section

Never peel the entire protective film off at once. Work in small sections — about 4–6 inches at a time. This keeps the canvas sticky and prevents drills from sticking to the wrong areas.

Use the right brightness for the time of day

In a bright room during the day, use a higher brightness setting. In the evening or in a darker room, turn the brightness down — the contrast between the lit canvas and the dark background makes the symbols very clear even at lower brightness.

Clean your light pad regularly

Wipe the surface of your light pad with a soft, dry cloth to keep it clean. Fingerprints and diamond wax can build up on the surface and reduce the clarity of the light.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I really need a light pad for diamond painting?

You do not need one — but once you try it, you will never go back. A light pad makes it so much easier to read symbols, reduces eye strain and speeds up the process significantly. It is one of the best upgrades for any diamond painter.

What size light pad do I need for diamond painting?

An A3 light pad (roughly 17 x 12 inches) is ideal for most diamond painting canvases. It is large enough to cover most designs while remaining compact enough for a desk setup.

Can I use a light pad for other crafts?

Absolutely! Light pads are also great for tracing, sketching, calligraphy, animation, scrapbooking and stained glass work. It is a very versatile tool for any creative hobby.

How long does the battery last on a rechargeable light pad?

Most rechargeable light pads last 4–6 hours on a single charge at medium brightness. You can also use them while charging if you need longer sessions.
