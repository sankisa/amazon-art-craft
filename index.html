Beginner's Buying Guide

Not sure where to start? We've got you covered.

🎨

Are You a Complete Beginner?

Start with a watercolor set or an all-in-one acrylic kit. They are forgiving, easy to clean, and budget-friendly. Avoid oil paints at first — they require solvents and long drying times.

→ See Basic Supplies
✂️

Into DIY & Crafts?

Go for embroidery floss, iron-on vinyl, or air-dry clay. These materials are versatile, affordable, and need no special equipment — just your hands and imagination.

🎁

Buying as a Gift?

You can't go wrong with a complete kit or bundle. They include everything the recipient needs and feel impressive to unbox. Pick one based on their hobby — knitting, painting, or diamond art.

→ See Kits & Bundles
💰

On a Tight Budget?

The embroidery floss kit and graphite pencil set are both under $15 and incredibly high value. You don't need expensive supplies to create beautiful art — skill matters more than price.

Buying for Kids?

Choose non-toxic options like air-dry clay or watercolors. Avoid products with small parts for children under 5. Kits with step-by-step instructions keep kids engaged and learning independently.

Painting Tips

5 Mistakes Beginners Make with Acrylic Paint (And How to Fix Them)

Acrylic paint dries fast — that's both its superpower and its biggest trap. The #1 mistake beginners make is using too little water, leading to streaky, unblended colors. Always keep a spray bottle nearby to keep your palette moist, and thin your paint slightly with water for smoother coverage. The second mistake is overworking the canvas — once acrylics start to dry, leave them alone and layer on top instead of pushing wet paint around.

Sketching Guide

How to Use Different Pencil Grades: From 6H to 6B Explained

Not all pencils are equal — and knowing which grade to use makes a huge difference in your drawings. H pencils (hard) produce light, crisp lines ideal for technical work and initial outlines. B pencils (soft) lay down rich, dark marks perfect for shading and expressive strokes. Start your sketch with a 2H for structure, build mid-tones with HB, and deepen shadows with 4B or 6B for dramatic contrast.

Craft Ideas

10 Easy Embroidery Projects You Can Finish in a Weekend

Embroidery doesn't have to mean complex patterns that take months to finish. Some of the most beautiful pieces are simple and fast — a floral hoop, a personalised name on a tote bag, or a tiny cactus on a denim jacket. All you need is a basic floss kit, a hoop, fabric, and a needle. Start with a backstitch outline, fill with satin stitch, and you'll have a finished piece in just a few hours.
